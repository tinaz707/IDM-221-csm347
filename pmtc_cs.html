PMTC Website Redesign

The Philadelphia Mar Thoma Church Redesigned Website provides information and encourages engagement of many different events, allowing for more user interactions!

Team:

Solo

Tool:

Figma

Role:

UX/UI Designer

Duration:

September 2024 - March 2025

Problem

The current website for the Mar Thoma Church of Philadelphia provides very little helpful information for current church members, such as myself, and is not a site many people find themselves returning to. Over the course of 6 months, I took it on myself to redesign the website to a more informative and welcoming site for members of my church.

Solution

A redesign! I focused on creating a responsive website that would be available on desktop, mobile, and tablet. My goals consisted of these:

  • Keep the User Flow Simple
  • Increase Interactive Elements
  • Adding Relevant Information

Research

Before I dove right into solving the problems the website had, I first had to analyze the current website and understand what exactly was going on. Here is what I found:

  • Lack of important information caused low engagement by current church members
  • Any information that was on the site was outdated

User Persona

Creating a User Persona, I wanted to have a basis for my users. Talking to many church members, I was able to gather a consensus on my target audience.

Designing

As I started my design process, I looked at the web and other church websites for inspiration. This allowed me to continuously make changes and edits to my wireframe. With each new change, I came to understand that I did not need to have a design similar to the original. It was then that I started to overcome my fear of whitespace..


When it came to choosing a color palette, I decided to use almost the same red hues as they are based on the Church's logo

Testing

After finishing up the design and animation for my prototype, I had three participants test out my prototype. First, I had each user interact with the original website and then my own redesign. After each experience, I had them note things about their experience.


With the feedback I was given, I was able to make further changes to my design! Ultimately, I went through 4 different wireframe designs before landing on the final one!

Outcome

This experience was my first big task in the field of UX/UI Design; I had many ups and downs. Now that I have gone through all this, I have learned some valuable things.


While having a personal connection to this project as a member of the church, I should have still focused more on the research first, rather than relying on what I knew. User testing actually led me to come to terms with some problems I overlooked, which made me make some design changes.


Rather than jumping straight into a wireframe, I should try to branch out my research, such as here, where I looked at other church websites for design inspirations.


Overall, with this project, I got a better idea of the UX Workflow as well as got into the tool Figma. I learned to look not only at the design but also at the usability. While this project is not perfect and has its mistakes, it was definitely a great learning experience I worked through.
